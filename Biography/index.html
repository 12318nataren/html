Eda Lovelace

December 10, 1815 - November 27, 1852

The intellectual, the moral, and the religious seem to be interrelated in a harmonious whole.

Time line

  • 1840
    Ada became addicted to horse racing and together with some of her friends they tried to create a mathematical model that would help them win big bets, but they failed.
  • 1843
    She published a series of notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine (describing concepts such as loop and subroutine) which she signed only with her initials for fear of being censored for being a woman.
  • June 5, 1833
    She had met Mary Somerville a famous author and scientist, later she introduced her to Charles Babbage (father of computers) and began an exchange of mathematical and logical ideas, he was so impressed with Ada's analytical ability that he named her "The lovely of numbers"
  • 1842
    In 1842 the Italian mathematician Louis Menebrea published a memoir in French on the Analytical Engine. Babbage enlisted Ada as the translator of the memory from French to English, and added a more voluminous set of notes from the original, which caused her to be considered the first female programmer in history.

In a nutshell

Born December 10, 1815, London, UK
Died November 27, 1852, Marylebone, London, UK
Education Young Ada receives math and science lessons in an attempt, by her mother, to eradicate the inheritance of poetic madness that was in her genes.

"Those who have learned to walk on the threshold of the unknown worlds, by means of what are often called par excellence the exact sciences, can then, with the beautiful white wings of imagination, hope to rise still higher in the uncharted world" by Eda Lovelace
